Online Education Is Waiting for a SuperHero

15 May, 2020
|

Start small — how about writing a business plan?

Project Positioning

SuperHero — Make students fall in love with learning! SuperHero — Gaming and learning were never opposites. Make kids learn like they play games — they just can’t stop! SuperHero — Knowledge is power. Come strengthen yourself to defeat the monsters! SuperHero — The killer of exam-oriented education. Drilling questions? We’ve got an even better system!

Market Pain Points

  1. Playing is in children’s nature. No matter how good current products on the market are, none of them fundamentally solve children’s resistance to learning. Without changing this, you can’t mobilize children’s initiative.

  2. Educators’ contributions only serve a small regional population. Teachers are essentially content creators, and their efforts deserve to be seen by more people — not just the students in their classroom. Excellent teachers need a bigger stage.

  3. Parents are the primary monetization source for education products. Under exam-oriented education, parents care about grades. Online education is still in its exploration phase and often struggles to improve student grades in a short time. Parents are reluctant to spend again.

Solutions

Cultivating Students’ Interest in Learning

  1. Learning makes you stronger. The main format is a level-based game where each level has monsters of corresponding difficulty, and the way to defeat them is by answering questions. It combines untimed and timed question modes — timed mode deals more damage. After clearing a level, there’s an in-depth review of all wrong answers. Each student’s progress data is stored, and the massive nationwide data can be used by algorithms to train better, more accurate questions to better serve every student!

Encourage kids to ask “why.” Knowledge Tree System Building connections between questions so children can progressively master the essence of each topic. After each level, there’s a bonus “follow-up” stage — passing it earns extra rewards. Likewise, wrong answers go into the error notebook. Q&A System Let children ask clearer questions and learn more thoroughly. 3. Learning doesn’t have to be boring. Quest system and storyline system. Changing Educators’ Traditional Teaching Mindset

Han Yu said, “A teacher transmits the Way, imparts knowledge, and resolves doubts.” Why is transmitting the Way listed first? What is the Way? Haha, this is where I want to digress a bit. I personally enjoy classical Chinese texts — not because I’m great at them, but because classical Chinese gives everyone a different space for imagination. Ancient Chinese literature is profound, with each character having multiple interpretations. It’s like watching a movie — everyone takes away something different. It’s the Rashomon effect: even after watching the same film, everyone understands it differently. People tend to see what they want to see. Okay, let me get back on track. The Way, in my understanding, means principles and methods. Applied to learning, it should be: how to learn? Why learn? So what should a teacher’s primary goal be? I think it aligns with our app’s ultimate goal: improving children’s ability to learn! Imparting knowledge and resolving doubts are more straightforward — teaching subject matter and answering questions. So how do we implement these three things with a modern teaching philosophy?

  1. Transmitting the Way

Embed the Way into the product. Teachers are the creators of levels, training camps, and premium courses. During creation, they can arrange different reward mechanisms for questions of increasing depth to encourage students to think deeper. Homework assignments can include slightly beyond-syllabus questions to prompt students to think and learn. The teacher-side app will provide prompts when designing questions: “Some thought-provoking and logic questions, though seemingly unrelated to the content, can spark students’ curiosity and desire to learn.” “The end of each level is a great moment to impart values to students. Seize it well — your words might influence their entire life!” 2. Imparting Knowledge

Course System 3. Resolving Doubts

Q&A System With data and algorithm support, more accurate information can be provided for each student, helping teachers better understand their students. There are also prompts before Q&A sessions that teachers can use as reference.

Recognition Drives Continued Spending

A parent-side app is definitely needed. Later, the payment system can be separated. The parent-side app can receive usage reports about their child to better understand them. Knowledge the child has learned gets reported back to parents. How could parents not be happy seeing their child grow? Thought-provoking questions are left for kids to ask their parents, promoting parent-child relationships. It’s okay if parents don’t know the answer — the parent-side app will have hints, so parents and children can progress together. Isn’t that wonderful?

Spending becomes a natural outcome — if the child genuinely improves through the app, parents won’t be as resistant to paying.

Food for thought: Should we also tackle adult education? Using the parent-side app?

Student App Introduction

Check-in System

Purpose

  • Earn basic items, boost combat power / accumulate game coins to exchange for premium questions or expert Q&A sessions
  • Earn achievements
  • For children, making progress every day increases app stickiness

Format

Answer questions to check in, with solutions displayed. Must read for a certain duration before claiming rewards.

Dungeon System

Purpose

Core app feature. Due to question type limitations, mostly basic questions focused on building a solid foundation.

Types

  • Question-based Levels
    1. Each question is a monster
    2. Choose between timed and untimed modes; timed rewards are richer
    3. Bonus questions — answering correctly triggers a critical hit
    4. Wrong answers cost HP; when HP is low, you must answer difficult survival questions or fail
  • Special Event Days
    1. World boss or special events, preceded by corresponding training camps. Timed questions, with rewards based on number of correct answers
    2. Holiday bosses and special question events. Correct answers earn rewards
  • Subject Categories
    1. Arts/Science split begins in high school
  • Grade Categories (Difficulty)
    1. Not limited by age; determined by ability

Achievement System

Purpose

  • Give children motivation to keep learning!
  • Satisfy children’s sense of accomplishment and competitive spirit

Types

  • Item collection
  • Titles
  • Dungeon completion records
    1. Completion rate
    2. Speed runs

Q&A System
